How to Book a Hotel — Practical Guide

Most hotel pricing is more flexible than it looks. A few rules cover 90% of the situations.

When to book

3–4 weeks out is the sweet spot for domestic US travel. Earlier than that, rates haven't been "yielded" yet (hotels release inventory in waves). Later than 14 days, prices climb steadily as availability shrinks. Same-day deals exist but you give up choice.

Tuesday and Wednesday have the lowest published rates for most business-traveler markets. Weekends are typically 20–40% higher in destination cities, the opposite in business cities like Nashville.

Conventions and major events 2-3x baseline rates. Check the city's convention calendar before assuming a date is normal-priced.

Cancellation: read the fine print

There are three common policies and they're not all created equal:

  • Free cancellation — usually free until 24-48 hours before check-in. After that, full charge. Best for uncertain plans.
  • Non-refundable / "Saver" rate — typically 10-20% cheaper. Locked in. No refunds, no changes. Only safe if your travel is 100% confirmed.
  • Pay at hotel — book now, pay on arrival. Lets you cancel without prepayment friction. Slight risk of overbooking on the hotel's end.

Loyalty programs (worth it for 3+ stays/year)

  • Hilton Honors — free to join, points on every stay. Status starts at Silver after 4 stays. 10 points per dollar.
  • Marriott Bonvoy — broadest portfolio (St. Regis, Westin, Sheraton, Marriott, Courtyard, Residence Inn, etc.). 10 points/dollar; status at 25 nights.
  • IHG One Rewards — Holiday Inn, InterContinental, Kimpton, Six Senses. Strong free-night certificates with credit-card pairing.
  • World of Hyatt — fewer hotels, but the points have the highest cents-per-point value of any major program.
  • Choice Privileges — Comfort Inn, Quality Inn, Cambria. Massive footprint, lower-tier rewards but easy to redeem.

If you book direct with the hotel's brand site (or via their app), you'll typically get the loyalty rate AND the points. Booking through a third-party usually disqualifies you from points and may cost you status credit.

Direct vs. Booking.com vs. Hotels.com vs. Expedia

For published rates, the major OTAs are usually within $1-3 of each other and within a few dollars of the brand site. Where they differ:

  • Direct (brand site or app): best for loyalty points + flexible cancellation. Some brands offer a "best rate guarantee" that matches OTA pricing.
  • Booking.com: largest inventory of independent and boutique properties. Strong filtering. Genius loyalty discount (10-20%) once you're qualified.
  • Hotels.com: Reward Nights program — every 10 nights booked = 1 free. Useful for frequent leisure travelers.
  • Expedia: bundles flight + hotel + car — sometimes cheaper as a package than booked separately.
  • Priceline: "Express Deals" hide the hotel name until you book — cheaper but you give up control.

Hidden fees to watch for

  • Resort fees — $25-45/night, mandatory, often not in the headline rate. Common in Vegas, Hawaii, beach resorts.
  • Parking — $25-60/night in major cities. Always check before assuming it's free.
  • Wi-Fi — usually free for loyalty members; sometimes $10-15/night for non-members.
  • Early check-in / late check-out — typically free if the hotel has availability and you ask politely. Brand status often unlocks it automatically.
