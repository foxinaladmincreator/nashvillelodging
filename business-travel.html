Best Hotels for Business Travel in Nashville

If you're in Nashville for work, where you stay depends on which corridor you're working in. This is the practical breakdown.

Nashville sprawls. A hotel that's 5 minutes from one office park is 45 minutes from another. The four districts below cover where most business travel demand sits — pick the one closest to your meetings.

Downtown CBD

Music City Center convention complex, Tennessee state government offices, and the financial district. Walking distance to most attractions. Hotels here run premium during conventions.

Anchors: Music City Center, AT&T Building, Bridgestone Tower

See Downtown CBD hotels

Cool Springs / Brentwood

I-65 South corridor, ~20 minutes from downtown. Healthcare HQs (Tractor Supply, AllianceBernstein, Mars Petcare). Quieter, suburban, typically 30% cheaper than downtown.

Anchors: CoolSprings Galleria, Tractor Supply HQ

See Cool Springs / Brentwood hotels

MetroCenter / North Nashville

A repurposed business park north of downtown. State of TN offices, Schermerhorn Plaza, and a growing roster of healthcare and logistics tenants.

Anchors: MetroCenter Boulevard, TN Tower

See MetroCenter / North Nashville hotels

BNA Airport / Donelson

Hotels along Murfreesboro Pike and Elm Hill Pike serve early morning flights and event traffic. 12 minutes from downtown by car.

Anchors: Nashville International (BNA), Opry Mills

See BNA Airport / Donelson hotels

Booking tips for business travelers

  • Book direct with brand programs if you're loyalty-eligible — Hilton Honors, Marriott Bonvoy, IHG One Rewards. The point earnings + status credit usually beats any 3rd-party discount.
  • Cancellation flexibility matters more than rate — meeting schedules shift. Filter for free cancellation when comparing.
  • Watch for resort fees and parking charges. Some hotels in business districts add $25–45/night in mandatory fees that aren't in the headline rate.
  • Wi-Fi quality varies wildly — premium-tier rooms often include faster Wi-Fi as a built-in amenity. Worth $10–20/night if you're on calls.
