Nashville Hotels — Common Questions

The things travelers ask most before booking in Nashville.

How close can I stay to Broadway in Nashville?

Plenty of hotels sit within a few blocks of Lower Broadway — downtown and SoBro (South of Broadway) put you in walking distance of the honky-tonks, the Ryman, and Bridgestone Arena. Just know downtown stays loud well into the night; light sleepers may prefer The Gulch or Germantown, both a short ride away.

Where do bachelorette parties usually stay in Nashville?

Most groups base themselves downtown or in SoBro to be walking distance from Broadway, or in The Gulch for a slightly calmer scene that's still close. Larger groups often book a block of rooms or a short-term rental in East Nashville or Germantown.

Is it too loud to sleep downtown in Nashville?

Downtown and SoBro can be noisy on weekend nights — Broadway runs late. Newer hotels have good soundproofing, but if quiet matters, The Gulch, 12 South, Germantown, or East Nashville give you a calmer night and a 5–10 minute ride into the action.

Do I need a car in Nashville?

Not if you're staying downtown and sticking to Broadway, the Ryman, and The Gulch — that's all walkable. To reach East Nashville, 12 South, the Grand Ole Opry, or day trips, you'll want a car or rideshare; there's no rail transit.

When is the cheapest time to book a hotel in Nashville?

Rates in Nashville are usually lowest about three to four weeks out, and mid-week check-ins (Tuesday and Wednesday) tend to beat weekends. Prices spike during major conventions and events, so check the city's event calendar before locking in a date.

Which area of Nashville should I stay in?

It depends on your trip — Downtown / Lower Broadway, The Gulch, Music Row are among the most popular places to base yourself. See our neighborhood guide for what each area is known for and where to stay in each.

Do Nashville hotels charge resort fees or parking fees?

Some do. Resort fees ($25–45/night) and parking ($20–60/night at city-center hotels) are often not included in the headline rate, so check the full price breakdown before booking. Many hotels waive parking for loyalty members.
