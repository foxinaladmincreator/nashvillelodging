Where to Stay in Nashville

Nashville isn't one experience — it's six or seven. Pick the neighborhood that matches your trip, then compare hotel prices in that area.

This guide covers the main hotel-dense neighborhoods in Nashville, TN, what each is known for, and what kind of trip each one is best for. Click any "See hotels" button to compare live prices on Booking.com filtered to that area.

Downtown / Lower Broadway

The honky-tonks, the Ryman Auditorium, the Country Music Hall of Fame, Bridgestone Arena. Loud and crowded any night of the week, especially weekends. Walking distance to most major attractions.

Best for: First-time visitors, bachelorette parties, music tourism

See hotels in Downtown / Lower Broadway

The Gulch

A LEED-certified neighborhood between downtown and Music Row. Modern condos, walkable, packed with restaurants (Biscuit Love, Whiskey Kitchen) and the iconic "What Lifts You" wings mural.

Best for: Weekend travelers, foodies, design-forward stays

See hotels in The Gulch

Music Row

The historic music industry hub — RCA Studio B (where Elvis recorded), publishing houses, and songwriter haunts. Less nightlife than downtown but iconic for music fans. Within walking distance of Vanderbilt.

Best for: Music fans, industry visitors, Vanderbilt-area trips

See hotels in Music Row

East Nashville

Across the Cumberland River from downtown. Indie record stores, dive bars, restaurants like Bolton's and The Pharmacy. Five Points is the neighborhood center. More residential, less touristed.

Best for: Locals' Nashville, design-conscious travelers, music lovers

See hotels in East Nashville

12 South

A walkable strip of boutiques (Draper James, White's Mercantile), restaurants, and the "I Believe in Nashville" mural. South of downtown, quieter than the Gulch, photo-friendly.

Best for: Shopping, photo-friendly weekends, slower-paced trips

See hotels in 12 South

Germantown

Historic district north of downtown. Restored 19th-century buildings, restaurants like Rolf and Daughters and City House, the Nashville Farmers Market. Slower pace, walkable, dinner-focused.

Best for: Dining, historic walks, leisure travelers
